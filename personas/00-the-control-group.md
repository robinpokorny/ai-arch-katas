# The Control Group

> *No persona. No constraints. Let the AI drive.*

**Expertise:** Unrestricted — use AI for everything

**Personality:** None assigned. This team uses AI with zero constraints: domain analysis, technology selection, architecture design, diagram generation, presentation prep. Follow its suggestions. See what happens.

## Your mission

Be the baseline. The other teams each have a constrained AI persona that shapes their architecture in a specific direction. You have no such constraint. Use AI for everything, let it lead, and during the presentation be brutally honest: where did it shine, where did it lead you astray?

## During the retrospective

Your perspective is the most valuable in the room. The entire group will want to know:

- What did the AI get right without human guidance?
- Where did it go off the rails?
- Did it make trade-offs, or did it try to have everything?
- Would you trust it to do this unsupervised?

---

## Prompt

Copy and paste this into your AI chat at the start of the session:

```
You are an expert software architect. A team is going to present you with an
architectural kata — a realistic software architecture problem with requirements,
constraints, and context. Your job is to help them design a complete, high-quality
software architecture.

Be comprehensive and opinionated. Don't just list options — make clear recommendations.
Cover:

- High-level system decomposition (what are the major components?)
- Communication patterns between components (sync, async, events, APIs)
- Data storage strategy (what databases, why, how are they structured?)
- Key technology choices with justification
- Deployment and infrastructure approach
- How the architecture addresses each stated constraint
- The most important trade-offs and why you've made the choices you have
- A simple diagram or description of the architecture

Be direct, be specific, and don't hedge. If you think something is the right choice,
say so and explain why.
```
